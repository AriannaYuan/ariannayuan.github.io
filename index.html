About

I'm a PhD student in cognitive psychology at Stanford University. I am also doing a PhD minor in Computer Science. My primary advisor is Prof. James McClelland. I'm also collaborating with the Stanford NLP group and Prof. Bruce McCandliss in the School of Education.
During the summer of 2017, I join Microsoft Research at Cambridge as a research intern. I work closely with Nate Kushman in The Machine Intelligence and Perception (MIP) group and Alex Taylor in the Human Experience and Design (HXD) group on dialog modeling.
My PhD research focuses on how children (and adults) learn mathematics through perceptual and embodied experience. I'm also curious about the interaction between natural language comprehension and mathematical cognition. To these ends, I build dialog agents that learn mathematics through interaction with simulated physical world and human(or machine) teachers.
